Places To Visit

Damro Arunachal Pradesh

The longest hanging bridge in Arunachal Pradesh at Damro sways gently over a silvery slash of the Yamne River as Adi Padam herders head to the forests with their mithun. Just above the woods, surrounded by terraced fields, is Yamne Abor, a cluster of thatched bamboo houses in a clearing.

Ganga Ghats at varanasi

Ghats in Varanasi are beautiful riverfront steps leading to the banks of the River Ganges

Gurez Valley In Kashmir

With waterfalls tumbling down slopes that are surrounded by snow-capped peaks and lush emerald fields, Jammu and Kashmir’s Gurez Valley is a hidden paradise plucked straight out of a whimsical folktale..

Lunglei

Lunglei, literally meaning 'bridge of rock' got its name from a bridge like rock found in the riverine area around Nghasih - a small tributary of Tlawng the longest river in Mizoram .

Silent Valley National Park

Silent Valley National Park is situated in the core of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve. The park is a rich storehouse of biodiversity. The Park is famous for its unique and diverse wildlife and bird species. ... Silent Valley is home to the largest population of Lion tailed macaques, an endangered of primate.

champawat

Lohaghat is an admired destination in the Champawat district of Uttarakhand state. Lohaghat holds immense historical and mythological importance and it is situated on the banks of Lohawati River at an altitude of 1745 mts above sea level.

dal lake in kashmir

The serene Dal Lake is one of the most beautiful places in India to visit before you die. Also known as the Lake of Flowers or Srinagar’s Jewel, it is 1775 m above sea level, and a shikara ride on the lake can lift your spirits instantly.

Pangong Tso

Pangong Tso is popular for its changing colour ability. Its colour changes from shades of blue to green to red. It is one of the highest altitude lakes in the world that is filled with saline water. It is located at an altitude of 4350 m above the sea level.
